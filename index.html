User Interface (UI)

Adobe After Effects is a digital visual effects, motion graphics, and compositing application. When you first launch After Effects, you'll be greeted with its user interface. Although it may seem complex at first, it is designed to provide a powerful and customizable workspace for video compositing and animation.

Main Elements in the Interface:

  1. Project Panel - This panel, usually located on the left side, is where all imported media (video, audio, images, etc.) and created assets are stored and organized.
  2. Composition Panel - Located in the center, this is where you can preview your video in real time.
  3. Timeline Panel - Located at the bottom, this panel is where you arrange and animate your layers, add keyframes, and manage the timing of your project.
  4. Preview Panel - The Preview panel controls the playback of your composition, allowing you to play, pause, and adjust the preview settings.
  5. Effects & Presets - This panel, usually found on the right, contains a library of effects and presets that can be applied to layers within your composition.

3) Timeline Panel:

Timeline: The Timeline Panel displays and manages the arrangement, animation, and timing of layers in your composition, with keyframes and layer properties accessible for editing.


4) Preview Panel:

Playback Controls: The Preview Panel controls the playback of your composition, allowing you to play, pause, and adjust preview settings like resolution and frame rate.


5) Effects & Presets:

Effects Library: The Effects & Presets panel contains a collection of effects and animation presets that can be applied to layers, providing tools for enhancing and modifying your composition.
