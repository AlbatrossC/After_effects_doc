Basic Terms in Adobe After Effects

1) Composition (Comp):

A container that holds layers, where you arrange and animate your media elements. Compositions can be nested within other compositions.

2) Layer:

An individual element within a composition, such as text, images, videos, or shapes. Layers can be stacked and manipulated independently.

3) Timeline:

The panel where you control the timing and sequencing of layers within a composition. It shows the duration of your composition and allows you to animate properties over time.

4) Keyframe:

A marker used to define the start and end points of a transition or animation. Keyframes are used to animate properties such as position, scale, and opacity.

5) Effect:

A pre-built or custom filter applied to layers to alter their appearance or behavior. Effects can be used to create animations, apply color corrections, and more.

6) Precomposition:

A technique where you group multiple layers into a single composition. This is useful for managing complex scenes and reusing compositions.

7) Mask:

A shape or path used to hide or reveal parts of a layer. Masks can be used to create effects like vignettes or to animate specific areas of a layer.

8) Rendering:

The process of exporting your composition to a final video file format. Rendering combines all the layers and effects into a single video output.

9) Footage:

The raw media files (such as video clips, images, or audio) imported into After Effects for use in compositions.

10) Animation Preset:

A pre-configured set of animation settings that can be applied to layers to quickly create specific animations or effects.

11) Expressions:

Scripts written in JavaScript that automate animations and effects. Expressions can link properties, create complex animations, and respond to user input.

12) Adjustment Layer:

A layer that applies effects to all layers below it in the composition. Adjustment layers are useful for applying global effects like color grading.
